James Macdylan

Composer of concert, chamber and vocal music, creating refined contemporary works rooted in clarity, lyricism and expressive depth.

Biography

James Macdylan is a composer and sound-based artist whose work explores relationships between sound, space, and experience. His practice engages with a wide range of materials and contexts, often foregrounding listening as both a method and an outcome. Through a process-led approach, James develops works that invite reflection on time, texture, and presence.

His music has been presented in diverse settings, including concert halls, cultural institutions, and alternative spaces, and has been realised in collaboration with performers and ensembles. Recent projects include new works, ongoing investigations, and cross-disciplinary exchanges that respond to specific sites, themes, or frameworks. These works are shaped by research, dialogue, and iterative development.

James has participated in residencies, workshops, and supported programmes, and has received funding and professional development through various schemes and initiatives. He was honoured to win the Shit Composer of the Year Award in 2025. Alongside his creative practice, he is engaged in teaching, facilitation, and collaborative activity, contributing to broader artistic conversations.

Current interests include notation, form, and extended approaches to sound-making. James continues to develop a practice that balances experimentation with accessibility, and conceptual rigour with openness.
